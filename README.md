1.1 Brief History
Nommy's Cleaning Service was founded in 2026 by Nommy, a passionate entrepreneur with a strong background in domestic and commercial cleaning services. Recognising the growing demand for reliable, professional, and affordable cleaning solutions in the Durban area, Nommy established the business to fill a gap in the market for high-quality cleaning services that combine trustworthiness with exceptional attention to detail.
Starting as a small residential cleaning operation, the business quickly gained a reputation for thoroughness, reliability, and warm customer service. With a focus on building long-term relationships with clients, Nommy's Cleaning Service is positioned for steady growth and expansion into the broader KwaZulu-Natal region.
The company is committed to using environmentally friendly cleaning products and employing well-trained, vetted staff who take pride in their work. Nommy's Cleaning Service is a proud member of the local small business community and is registered with relevant South African small business development bodies.

3. Current Business Analysis
Nommy's Cleaning Service is a new startup with the following analysis conducted to inform business strategy and growth planning.
3.1 Strengths
· Founder has hands-on cleaning experience and deep understanding of client expectations
· Low startup costs with minimal overheads required
· Flexible service offerings (residential, commercial, ad-hoc)
· Eco-friendly cleaning products are a unique selling point in the local market
· Personalised, one-on-one customer service from the owner
· Affordable pricing compared to larger, established cleaning companies
· Strong local network in Durban for word-of-mouth marketing
3.2 Weaknesses and Areas for Improvement
· No established brand recognition or client base yet
· Limited marketing budget for large-scale advertising
· No website or online booking system currently in place
· Lack of formal business registration and insurance (to be addressed)
· Limited staff — initially a one-person operation
· No dedicated vehicle for transporting equipment
· Not yet listed on popular online directories or review platforms
3.3 Opportunities
· Growing demand for professional cleaning services in Durban's residential and commercial sectors
· Partnership opportunities with real estate agencies and property managers
· Expansion into corporate cleaning contracts with offices and retail spaces
· Potential to offer specialised services (carpet cleaning, upholstery, window cleaning, deep sanitisation)
· Use of social media and online advertising to reach a wider audience
· Subscription-based cleaning packages for recurring revenue
· Collaboration with Airbnb/guesthouse owners for turnover cleaning services
· Government and small business development grants for women-owned enterprises
3.4 Threats
· Competition from established cleaning companies in Durban
· Price competition from informal / unregistered cleaners
· Economic downturn affecting clients' ability to pay for cleaning services
· High staff turnover in the cleaning industry if the business expands
· Safety concerns when entering clients' private properties
· Rising costs of cleaning supplies and transportation
